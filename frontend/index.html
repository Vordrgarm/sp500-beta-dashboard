📐 Our calculation method
We compute Beta using OLS regression of daily stock returns against SPY (S&P 500 ETF) returns over the selected period. Formula: β = Cov(Rᵢ, Rₘ) / Var(Rₘ) — where Rᵢ are daily stock returns and Rₘ are daily SPY returns.
📊 Yahoo Finance method
Yahoo Finance calculates Beta using monthly returns over 5 years vs the S&P 500 index, sourced from Refinitiv. Selecting 5 years above makes our calculation most comparable to theirs.
🔍 Delta column
Delta = |β calculated − β Yahoo|. Small deltas (<0.1) confirm alignment. Larger deltas reflect differences in return frequency (daily vs monthly) and market proxy (SPY ETF vs S&P 500 index).

Beta interpretation guide
β > 1.2 — Aggressive
Moves more than the market. Higher risk, higher potential return.
β 0.8–1.2 — Neutral
Moves roughly in line with the market.
β < 0.8 — Defensive
Moves less than the market. Lower risk, lower volatility.
β < 0 — Inverse
Moves opposite to the market. Rare in S&P 500 stocks.
