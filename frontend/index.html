📐 How we calculate Beta
Beta measures how much a stock moves relative to the overall market. We use the formula β = Cov(Rᵢ, Rₘ) / Var(Rₘ), where Rᵢ is the stock's monthly return, Rₘ is the S&P 500 index monthly return, and Cov / Var captures how much the stock's movements are explained by market movements. We use 5 years of monthly data — the same window used by Yahoo Finance.
📊 How Yahoo Finance calculates Beta
Yahoo Finance uses the same 5-year monthly return methodology, but sources its price data from Refinitiv, a professional financial data provider. Small differences in how dividends and corporate actions are adjusted produce minor discrepancies — these are data source differences, not calculation errors.

Beta interpretation guide
β > 1.2 — Aggressive
Moves more than the market. If the market drops 10%, this stock typically drops more than 12%.
β 0.8–1.2 — Neutral
Moves roughly in line with the overall market.
β < 0.8 — Defensive
Moves less than the market. Provides cushion during downturns.
β < 0 — Inverse
Tends to rise when the market falls. Rare in the S&P 500.
